1. Brief Conclusion Summarizing Learning Outcomes

This lab provided practical experience in vision-based robot control using ROS 2 and OpenCV. Image data from the onboard camera was processed to detect a colored object, determine its position, and generate appropriate motion commands. The implementation demonstrated how perception data can be converted into control actions through a closed-loop feedback system. Students gained experience with image processing, object detection, ROS 2 communication, and controller tuning.

2. Conclusion

The Vision-Based Target Tracking Lab successfully demonstrated the integration of perception and control in mobile robotics. Using ROS 2, OpenCV, and the TurtleBot3 simulation environment, a robot was able to detect, track, and approach a colored target autonomously. The lab highlighted the importance of image processing, color segmentation, centroid calculation, and proportional control in real-time robotic applications. Overall, the exercise strengthened understanding of how sensor information can be used to make intelligent navigation decisions in autonomous mobile robots.
